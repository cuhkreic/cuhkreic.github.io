About REIC

A well-designed platform for communication between the commercial society and CUHK students.

Established in 2012, Real Estate Investment Club (REIC) is a booming club in the Chinese University of Hong Kong with a focus on real estate industry.

So far, REIC has established concrete and long lasting cooperation with leading real estate companies by conducting research projects and carrying out activities. Backed by about 15,000 CUHK students and 154,000 alumni, REIC has attracted 446 members from HK, Mainland and overseas up to 2015/16 academic year. REIC will continue to evolve with professional attitude and great support from members and company partners.

CBRE Research

CBRE Research

Project Description

REIC has cooperated with CBRE for 2 years. In 2014-2015, we had a research project under the topic of Chinese Investors' Behaviour in Spanish Real Estate Market. In 2013---2014, REIC had two successful research projects with CBRE, covering topics as greater China investors with regard to Hong Kong real estate market and high---end retail market status and prospective in Hong Kong and mainland China. This year, REIC is dedicated to keep the collaborative relationship with CBRE and continually bring on research projects.

By exploring the future of the real estate market in the Greater China, the participants gained hands---on experience with top real estate strategists and some were awarded a part---time intern in CBRE. During the sharing session at the end of the projects, members said they got to admire this company’s expertise and culture and regard CBRE as one of their ideal employers.

KaiLong
KaiLong Project

KaiLong Project

Project Description

Based on past cooperation with KaiLong, in 2014, REIC launched a research about investment immigrants.

After careful screening, REIC selected passionate members to work with the project. Based on the guideline provided by KaiLong, team members did research on each stage and met weekly to share and discuss the analysis, as well as drawing schemes for the next steps. REIC is also honored to have these projects led and guided by the Managing Director Mr. Ho. Through bi-weekly guidance meetings or conference calls, members were better equipped with hands-on experience about professional real estate investment research.

REIT
REIT Project

REIT Project

Project Description

2014 is the first year that REIC collaborated with Champion REIT. The topic of the research is about acquisition analysis. With the efforts of the research team, the support and guidance from Champion, the project worked out successfully.

After careful screening, ten dedicated and passionate undergraduates and graduates were selected to participate in this project. Through weekly meetings within the groups and bi---weekly meetings with Champion specialists, they prepared research guidelines, conducted qualitative and quantitative research, worked out final report and gave presentation to the company’s management.

After the successful collaboration, in 2015 REIC continued to conduct a research project with Champion REIT. This time, REIC team focused on the effect of e- commerce wave on traditional retail industry, analyzed the market situation Langham Place, the shopping mall the Champion REIT owned, is faced with in the O2O wave and proposed a developing plan for Langham Place. This project is an exploration of REIC in new fields. Champion REIT spoke highly of our final proposal and regarded it to be “creative and inspirational”.

Lasalle Project

Project Description

REIC has been collaborated with LaSalle on research projects for 3 years. Participants analyzed on the major driving force of real estate market of China in 2012 and focused on the investment trends of China high net worth individuals in 2013. In 2014, we made a research about Continuing Care Retirement Community Investment in China.

After careful screening, REIC selected dedicated and passionate members to work these two projects. Based on the guideline provided by lasalle, team members did research on each stage and met weekly to share and discuss the analysis, as well as drawing schemes for the next steps. REIC is also honored to have these projects led and guided by professional LaSalle strategists, Mr. Paul Guest (Head of Asia Pacific), Ms. Catherine Chen (Head of Greater China) and Mr. Lawrence Ye (Associate). Through bi---weekly guidance meetings or conference calls, members were better equipped with hands---on experience about professional real estate investment research
